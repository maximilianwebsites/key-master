1. How to find the Notes (Sharps & Flats)

The Sharps Rhyme: Father Charles Goes Down And Ends Battle

  • Go exactly one half-step DOWN from the key you want. That note is your LAST sharp.
  • Example for A Major: Half-step down is G#. Say the rhyme up to G: F, C, G. The sharps are F#, C#, G#.

The Flats Rhyme: Battle Ends And Down Goes Charles' Father

  • Find your key in the rhyme, then go ONE FLAT PAST IT. That is your last flat.
  • Example for Eb Major: Find E (Battle, Ends...), go one past it (And = A). The flats are Bb, Eb, Ab.
  • Exception: F Major has one flat (Bb).

For Minor Keys: every minor key shares its key signature with a "relative major" found 3 half-steps UP from the minor root. Work out that major key's sharps/flats using the rhymes above, then simply start (and end) the scale on the minor root instead.

  • Example for E Minor: 3 half-steps up from E is G. G Major has one sharp (F#). So E Minor is E, F#, G, A, B, C, D — same sharp, new starting note.

2. How to find the Chords

Every major key follows the exact same pattern of major, minor, and diminished chords. Just apply this formula to the 7 notes you figured out in Step 1:

  • 1st: Major
  • 2nd: minor (add 'm')
  • 3rd: minor (add 'm')
  • 4th: Major
  • 5th: Major
  • 6th: minor (add 'm')
  • 7th: diminished (add 'dim')

Every natural minor key follows its own consistent pattern instead:

  • 1st: minor (add 'm')
  • 2nd: diminished (add 'dim')
  • 3rd: Major
  • 4th: minor (add 'm')
  • 5th: minor (add 'm')
  • 6th: Major
  • 7th: Major
